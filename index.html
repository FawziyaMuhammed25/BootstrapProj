A work of fiction is created in the imagination of its author. The author invents the story and makes up the characters, the plot or storyline, the dialogue and sometimes even the setting. A fictional work does not claim to tell a true story

Put simply, a classic novel is a book that has stood the test of time because it's so good; it has a gripping story which is expertly crafted and brilliantly expressed. But of course, it's not just about the story. Like any good recipe, there are a number of key ingredients that make a book a classic

Historical fiction is a literary genre where the story takes place in the past. Historical novels capture the details of the time period as accurately as possible for authenticity, including social norms, manners, customs, and traditions
